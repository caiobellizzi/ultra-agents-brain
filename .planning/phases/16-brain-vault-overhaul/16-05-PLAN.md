Key iCloud APFS constraint: shutil.move() and os.rename() fail silently or leave ghost copies on iCloud Drive APFS volumes because iCloud intercepts the rename syscall. The safe pattern is:
  dest.write_bytes(src.read_bytes())
  src.unlink()
This is a copy-then-delete, which bypasses the iCloud interception layer.

The inbox_sweep.py sweep() function already has this pattern at lines 335-336 and 341-342, but the issue is that _update_frontmatter_telos() calls item.write_text() before the move, and on iCloud the file may not have flushed. The fix: after write_text(), call item.read_bytes() to force a flush before the write_bytes+unlink sequence.

ultra_brain/monitor.py run_poll() uses shutil.move() at lines 168 and 172 (for items with score >=0.6 going to articles, and score <0.3 going to auto-culled). Replace both with write_bytes+unlink.
</context>
